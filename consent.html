PRINCIPAL INVESTIGATOR: Lauren Emberson, Ph.D.

 

Your son/daughter is being invited to take part in a research study. Before you decide about your child’s participation in this study, it is important that you understand why the research is being done and what it will involve. Please take the time to read the following information carefully. Please email the researcher if there is anything that is not clear or if you need more information.

Purpose of the research: The purpose of this study is to better understand how perception and learning interrelate to support lifespan development.

Study Procedures: This study will take 5 minutes-20 minutes. Typically, studies are 5 minutes-20 minutes long including time for full explanation of the purpose and procedure. During this time your child may be shown different visual images either individual objects (either familiar to your child or novel), faces or other pictures (e.g., of indoor and outdoor scenes). Your child may also watch videos (e.g., TV shows, movies, or animations of objects). Your child may see these images in combination with different auditory stimuli including speech (a familiar language, a foreign language or speech sounds that you haven’t heard before), musical tones, or novel sounds (e.g., rattles and synthesizer sounds). While watching various combinations of these auditory and visual stimuli your child may be required to respond to small variations in these stimuli (e.g., if a picture is presented upside down), to respond when you see a particular stimulus (e.g., whenever you see a smiling face) or to discriminate different stimuli (e.g., is this picture familiar to you or novel?). You or your child will use your keypad, mouse, or touch screen for these responses. You may also be asked to respond verbally to the experimenter.

* total expected time commitment for this study is: 5 minutes – 20 minutes

Audio Recordings: Your child (and possibly you in the background) may be voice-recorded during this procedure for research purposes. Laboratory staff will review the voice recording at a later time. Audio-recording is required for participation, and your child cannot participate if you do not agree to be recorded. Audio recordings will be kept in password-protected files without any of your personal information that are only accessible to authorized researchers only. Audio recordings will collected using the platform hosting the computer task, such as LabVanced, PsychoPy, jsPsych.

Benefits and Risks: There are no known physical or emotional risks involved in this study beyond those in everyday life.

Alternatives: There are no known alternatives available.

Confidentiality: All records/data from this study will be kept confidential. Your child’s responses will be kept private, and we will not include any information that will make it possible to identify you or your child in any report we might publish. Research records will be stored securely on password-protected computers. The research team will be the only party that will have access to your child’s data.
